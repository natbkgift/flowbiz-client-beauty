# Pilot Customer Plan - FlowBiz Beauty

Phase: 10 - Sales package

## Pilot Goal

Run a 14-30 day pilot with 3-5 friendly aesthetic clinics to validate workflow adoption and revenue opportunity.

The pilot is not a production rollout. It validates:

- Lead follow-up workflow
- Staff approval behavior
- No-show recovery
- Review request workflow
- Botox/Filler repeat reminders
- Audit trail usefulness
- Safety and operational fit

## Ideal Pilot Clinic Profile

Best-fit pilot clinics:

- Aesthetic, beauty, dermatology, or plastic surgery clinic
- Receives leads from Facebook, LINE, website, referral, or ads
- Has staff responsible for lead follow-up
- Has Botox/Filler or repeat-treatment revenue
- Has owner/manager willing to review weekly metrics
- Accepts that AI drafts require staff approval before sending
- Understands simulated/default integration limits during early pilot

Poor fit:

- No consistent lead flow
- No staff available to use the queue
- Wants full EMR/scheduling/inventory/payment system immediately
- Requires real external sends before staging QA
- Expects promised revenue outcome rather than measured workflow validation

## Qualification Criteria

Before accepting pilot:

- Lead volume: at least 50-100 inbound leads/month preferred
- Staff owner: named person responsible for daily usage
- Decision maker: owner or manager joins kickoff and final review
- Data readiness: can provide baseline estimates without real sensitive data if needed
- Safety agreement: no AI message is sent without staff approval
- Scope fit: agrees MVP is revenue automation layer, not EMR

## Pilot Setup Checklist

- Confirm pilot objective
- Confirm pilot dates: 14-30 days
- Choose 3-5 workflows from MVP set
- Identify staff users and roles
- Configure demo/staging tenant
- Confirm no-real-send policy unless separate QA plan exists
- Load safe demo or clinic-approved non-sensitive sample data
- Prepare message templates
- Explain HITL approval process
- Confirm weekly review schedule
- Define success metrics

## Week 0 - Onboarding

Activities:

- 30-minute owner/manager kickoff
- 45-minute staff workflow walkthrough
- Confirm current lead channels and baseline process
- Pick initial workflows:
  - New Lead Welcome
  - Uncontacted Lead Alert
  - No-Show Recovery
  - Review Request
  - Botox/Filler Reminder where relevant
- Explain AI safety and approval rules
- Confirm reporting cadence

Output:

- Pilot tenant ready
- Staff users ready
- Workflow list locked
- Baseline metrics recorded

## Week 1 - Usage Validation

Focus:

- Are staff opening the queue daily?
- Are AI suggestions understandable?
- Are approval/reject/modify actions clear?
- Are uncontacted leads visible?
- Are audit logs useful for manager review?

Review metrics:

- Number of leads reviewed
- Number of pending approvals
- Approval rate
- Rejection/modification reasons
- Staff usage frequency
- Uncontacted lead count

## Week 2 - Workflow Tuning

Focus:

- Improve templates and workflow timing
- Tune no-show recovery language
- Tune review request workflow
- Tune repeat reminder timing
- Remove workflows staff do not use

Review metrics:

- No-show recovery attempts
- Review request attempts
- Repeat reminder opportunities
- Staff time saved estimate
- Qualitative staff feedback

## Week 3-4 - Conversion To Paid

Focus:

- Summarize pilot results
- Compare baseline vs pilot workflow data
- Estimate monthly opportunity conservatively
- Confirm required integration setup
- Decide plan: Starter, Growth, Pro, or custom Enterprise

Decision outcomes:

- Convert to paid plan
- Extend pilot with narrower scope
- Pause due to low fit

## Success Metrics

Quantitative:

- Response time trend
- Uncontacted lead count
- Pending approval count
- AI approval rate
- AI modification rate
- No-show recovery attempts
- Review request attempts
- Repeat reminder opportunities
- Staff active days
- Estimated revenue opportunity

Qualitative:

- Staff says queue is useful
- Owner sees better visibility
- Approval process feels safe enough
- Templates fit clinic tone
- Workflow does not create extra operational burden

## Weekly Feedback Form

Ask owner/manager:

- What workflow was most useful this week?
- Where did staff get stuck?
- Did any AI suggestion feel risky or off-brand?
- Did the approval queue help or slow the team?
- Which lead/repeat opportunities were easier to see?
- What should be removed or simplified?
- What would need to be true to pay for this monthly?

Ask staff:

- Did you use the queue daily?
- Which message suggestions helped?
- Which suggestions did you reject or edit?
- Did the system save time?
- What was confusing?
- What would make this easier tomorrow?

## Support Process

During pilot:

- Shared support channel with clinic owner/manager
- Daily check-in during first 3 business days
- Weekly 30-minute review
- Issue log with owner, severity, status, next action
- Safety issue escalation path for AI/medical wording concerns

Severity:

- Critical: AI/outbound safety breach, tenant data issue, auth issue
- High: HITL approval broken, audit missing, workflow blocking staff
- Medium: copy/template mismatch, confusing UI, reporting gap
- Low: wording, training, preference changes

## Risk Handling

Stop pilot or pause workflow if:

- AI-generated text can be sent without approval
- Wrong tenant data appears
- Staff tries to use the system as medical advice
- Real external send is accidentally enabled
- Clinic requests production use before staging validation
- Patient/customer real data is handled without agreed consent process

## Conversion Criteria

Recommend paid conversion only when:

- Staff used FlowBiz repeatedly during pilot
- Owner/manager can identify clear operational value
- Safety rules were understood and followed
- At least one workflow produced measurable opportunity
- Required integrations are scoped honestly
- Pricing aligns with expected opportunity and support needs
